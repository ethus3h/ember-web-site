Key to non-flowering seed plants of Mount Desert Island, Maine. 

1a. abaxial surface of leaves whitish; branching pattern of twigs quite symmetrical.................Abies balsamea
1b. abaxial surface of leaves possibly whitish; branching pattern of twigs possibly symmetrical
     2a. leaves attached to sterigmata
          3a. twigs pubescent
               4a. twig ridges appear inflated......................................................Picea rubens
               4b. twig ridges do not appear inflated...............................................Picea mariana
          3b. twigs glabrous........................................................................Picea glauca
     2b. leaves not attached to sterigmata
          5a. abaxial surface of leaves whitish.....................................................Tsuga canadensis
          5b. abaxial surface of leaves not whitish
               6a. leaves in fascicles
                    7a. leaves 8 or more per fascicle...............................................Larix laricina
                    7b. leaves 2, 3, or 5 per fascicle
                         8a. leaves 2 per fascicle
                              9a. leaves approximately 10 centimetres in length.....................Pinus resinosa
                              9b. leaves approximately 5 centimetres in length
                                   10a. young bark brown/grey.......................................Pinus banksiana
                                   10b. young bark salmon...........................................Pinus sylvestris
                         8b. leaves 3 or 5 per fascicle
                              11a. leaves 3 per fascicle............................................Pinus rigida
                              11b. leaves 5 per fascicle............................................Pinus strobus
               6b. leaves not in fascicles
                    12a. leaves in whorls of three..................................................Juniperus communis
                    12b. leaves not in whorls of three
                         13a. leaves of two types
                              14a. leaves blue-green in summer, purple in winter....................Juniperus horizontalis
                              14b. leaves blue-grey in summer, blue-purple in winter................Juniperus horizontalis "Bar Harbour"
                         13b. leaves of one type....................................................Thuja occidentalis
