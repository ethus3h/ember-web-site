Notes:
  1. The horizontal lines between blocks of releases indicate major changes in style/ability etc.
  2. Sometimes, italicization of an attribute of an item (i. e., one of the comments regarding a list entry) has been used to distinguish items within the list in which italicization holds some form of semantic meaning, or in which for some reason italicization would be undesirable.
  3. This list uses Unicode to display its content, and assumes that support for its characters is fairly complete in your computer.
  4. This list assumes the presence of the following specialty typefaces in your computer for displaying text in Anoé (anoé/anoé) and Japanese, and for displaying other unusual characters:
    • AnoeyFuturamerlincom (version 2.61 or later)
    • AnoeyTuinelanFuturamerlincom (version 1.1 or later)
  5. In addition, the following Wreathe typeface family might be useful, with the  useful components in bold (the rest are provided for completeness) — using these typefaces will provide rudimentary support for all of Unicode with the exceptions of Kaithi, Sora Sompeng, Chakma, Sharada, Miao, and mathematical symbols for Arabic:
    • Wreathe (version 2.6F provided) (the main typeface) and its variants:
      • Wreathe Italic (version 2.0G provided) (Italic)
      • Wreathe Bold Italic (version 1 provided) (Bold Italic)
      • WreatheM (version 1 provided) (A variant of the Roman style)
      • Wreathe Decorative (version 1.11 provided) (A decorative version)
      • Wreathe SC (version 1 provided) (Small capitals)
    • Wreathe Unicode (version 3.14.2.1 provided) (a pan-Unicode typeface)
    • Wreathe Han A JP (version 1.0 provided) (a Han Unicode typeface)
    • Wreathe Han B JP (version 1.0 provided) (a Han Unicode typeface)
  6. Many of the computer files provided herein are in proprietary formats that may prove challenging to open. At some point I plan to provide all the files reëncoded using the Free Lossless Audio Codec, but I have not yet done that.
  7. The years of composition on some of the track listings shown in the artwork are often approximate.
  8. Unless otherwise noted, the first image in the album artwork is the cover.
  9. Unless otherwise noted, each piece was composed, performed, and produced by Elliot Chandler Wallace.
  10. How catalogue numbers work: The "Original series" catalogue numbers are the numbers assigned by the data HTML file corresponding to the release. The "New series" catalogue numbers are numbers assigned by artist, in the format x-n, where x is the artist ID, and n is the release ID (a rough estimate of the chronological order of the releases was used to establish the ordering of release IDs). For example, Futuramerlin.com is artist 6, so the first release under that artist name, the 17-track edition of Sylvan Fantasy, has the new series number 6-1. The "ID" is the new (assignments begun 8 October 2012) release numbering system, designed to not conflict with either earlier system.
