Parts of a flower.

 Description:

•(the flowers on a plant are known as the inflorescence)
•calyx: the sepals, considered collectively
•sepal: that which covers the flower when it is a bud
•corolla: the petals, considered collectively
•petal: the (usually brightly coloured) section of a flower that is, most often, used to attract insects, birds, or other pollinators
•andrœcium: the stamens of a flower, considered collectively
•stamen: a filament and an anther, considered collectively
•filament: a portion of a flower supporting an anther
•anther: a portion of a flower that carries pollen
•gynœcium: the pistil(s, considered collectively)
•pistil: a carpel or the fused carpels, considered collectively
•carpel: an ovary, a style, and a stigma, taken collectively
•ovary: the portion of a flower that contains the ovule
•ovule: the portion of a flower that contains the female reproductive cells
•style: the portion of a carpel between the ovary and the stigma
•stigma: the portion of a flower that receives pollen
