𝐈𝐦𝐩𝐨𝐫𝐭𝐚𝐧𝐜𝐞 𝐨𝐟 𝐑𝐞𝐩𝐮𝐭𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧, 𝑎𝑠 𝑒𝑚𝑏𝑜𝑑𝑖𝑒𝑑 𝑖𝑛 𝙼𝚞𝚌𝚑 𝙰𝚍𝚘 𝙰𝚋𝚘𝚞𝚝 𝙽𝚘𝚝𝚑𝚒𝚗𝚐․

     Reputation  is  a  major  force  in  the  lives  of  the  characters  in  this  play.    It  is  one  of  the  main  points  around  which  the  plot  is  built.  The  plot  surrounds  the  deception  of  the  suitor,  Claudio,  of  a  woman,  Hero,  by  slander,  causing  him  to  fail  to  marry  her:  at  the  wedding,  he  denounces  her  as  "more  intemperate  in  your  blood  than  Venus"[1].This  bad  reputation  causes  a  great  muddle.  He  is  led  to  believe  that  she  is  dead.  The  woman's  father  is  lead  to  believe  that  she  is  dead  as  well;  he  then  attempts  to  kill  Claudio,  mistakenly  thinking  that  Claudio  is  responsible  for  Hero's  death,  when  it  was  truly  Don  John,  Claudio's  ruler's  half  brother,  who  had  done  so.

      This  damage  to  reputation  has  most  certainly  demonstrated  its  importance.  Reputations  are  quite  valuable,  as  demonstrated  in  this  play.  They  are,  in  addition,  quite  as  easy  to  damage  or  destroy.  This  adds  to  their  value.
