Food Poisoning.

The lack of oversight and excessive speed of the lines in slaughterhouses can cause fecal contamination of the meat. This can lead to outbreaks of mutant strains of Escherichia coli. Some strains of this can create Shiga toxins, causing hemorrhagic colitis. One such strain, O157:H7, is one of the most common strains; the most probable number of E. coli O157:H7 is 67 organisms per uncooked patty[8]. From the mixing of many animals' meat, “a single animal infected with E. coli O157:H7 can contaminate thirty-two thousand pounds of ground beef”[11].
